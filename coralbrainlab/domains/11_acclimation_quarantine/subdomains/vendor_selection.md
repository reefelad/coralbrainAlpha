# Vendor Selection & Ethical Sourcing

## Overview
The long-term stability of a Triton-based reef system begins long before livestock enters the aquarium. Every organism introduced is a potential vector for pathogens, pests, and chemical instability. **Vendor Selection** is the process of auditing the "Chain of Custody" to ensure that the livestock's journey from the reef (or farm) to the display tank has prioritized biological integrity over commercial speed.

From a **Discovery First** perspective, the quality of a vendor is measured by the **physiological resilience** of their livestock. Organisms that have been handled correctly—maintained in stable water chemistry, provided with adequate nutrition, and spared from toxic ammonia spikes during transit—arrive with robust immune systems. In contrast, poorly handled specimens often suffer from "Late-Onset Mortality," where they appear healthy for days but succumb to internal organ failure or opportunistic infections weeks later. Choosing vendors who align with the Triton philosophy of stability ensures that new additions contribute to, rather than detract from, the system's equilibrium.

## Key Concepts

### The Chain of Custody
Every hand that touches a fish or coral adds a layer of stress. The ideal chain is as short as possible:
1.  **Direct Sourcing (Tier-1):** Vendors who collect their own livestock or buy directly from mariculture facilities. They have the most control over handling.
2.  **Landed Wholesalers (Tier-2):** Large-scale importers who receive shipments and stabilize them before selling to retailers.
3.  **Local Fish Stores (LFS) / Online Retailers (Tier-3):** Facilities that hold livestock for final sale. Their value lies in their local acclimation and observation periods.
[TIER-2: Industry Standard Practices]

### Ethical Sourcing Tiers
Sourcing impact relates directly to the sustainability of the hobby and the health of the specimens:
- **Captive-Bred / Aquacultured [TIER-1]:** Born and raised in closed systems. These are the "gold standard" as they are already adapted to aquarium life and carry the lowest pathogen risk. Examples: ORA, Sustainable Aquatics, Biota.
- **Maricultured [TIER-2]:** Grown in ocean-based nurseries from fragments of wild colonies. They support local island communities while reducing impact on natural reefs. Look for **MAC (Marine Aquarium Council) Certified** sources.
- **Sustainably Wild-Caught [TIER-2]:** Collected using net-only methods with CITES permits. Essential for species not yet bred in captivity.
- **Unverified Wild-Caught [TIER-3]:** Low-cost specimens with no handling history. High risk of cyanide use or poor post-capture care.
[TIER-1: CITES / NOAA Research / MASNA]

### Critical Resilience: The "Sensitive Species" Rule
While a hardy Damselfish might survive a poor vendor's chain of custody, high-risk or "Lazarus" species will not. Vendor selection is non-negotiable for:
- **Sensitive Fish:** Copperband Butterflies, Moorish Idols, Anthias species, and Dragonets.
- **High-End Corals:** Wild-caught Acropora colonies, deep-water Chalices, and sensitive NPS (Non-Photosynthetic) corals.
For these species, a vendor's "Dead on Arrival" (DOA) and "Stay Alive" guarantees are not just consumer protections; they are proxies for the vendor's confidence in their own husbandry protocols.
[TIER-2: Expert Consensus / ReefBuilders]

### Vendor Infrastructure Audit
A high-quality vendor should manifest "Stability over Numbers" in their own facility:
- **Dedicated Quarantine:** Separate systems for fish, corals, and invertebrates to prevent cross-contamination.
- **Prophylactic Treatment:** Use of copper (at therapeutic levels), hyposalinity, or specific dips before sale.
- **Nutritional Support:** High-frequency feeding of nutrient-dense foods (frozen mysis, enriched brine, high-quality pellets) to rebuild metabolic reserves.
- **Transparency:** Willingness to share arrival dates, origin, and current water parameters.
[TIER-2: BRS / ReefBuilders Expert Consensus]

## Practical Application

### The Vendor Audit Checklist
When evaluating a new vendor, ask the following:
1.  **"How long has this specimen been in your system?"** (Minimum 7-14 days for fish is preferred to allow for "Double Stress" recovery).
2.  **"What is your current salinity and pH?"** (Matches should be close to your own to reduce acclimation shock).
3.  **"Was this fish eating upon arrival?"** (Specifically ask what it is eating now to ensure successful transition).
4.  **"Do you use copper or UV in your holding systems?"** (Crucial for determining if pathogens are being suppressed or eliminated).

### Quality Indicators
- **DOA & Health Guarantees:** Look for vendors offering 14-day health guarantees. This indicates a higher level of confidence in their pre-sale conditioning.
- **Holding Density:** Lower fish density in sales tanks usually results in lower stress and fewer aggression-related injuries.
- **Acclimation Procedures:** Vendors who use slow drip or specialized receiving tanks for new arrivals are significantly better than those who "float and dump."

## Safety Considerations

⚠️ **TIER 1 CONSTRAINTS (Absolute):**
- **CITES Compliance:** Never purchase protected species without verifying legal CITES documentation. Illegal trade threatens the entire hobby's future and global reef health.
- **Cyanide-Free Guarantee:** Only buy from vendors who commit to net-caught or captive-bred fish. Cyanide destroys internal organs, leading to "unexplained" death 3-6 weeks after purchase.

⚠️ **TIER 2 DEFAULTS:**
- **Assume Every Wild Specimen is a Carrier:** Regardless of vendor claims, always treat wild-caught livestock as having potential parasites (Ich, Velvet, or Flukes) until quarantined by the aquarist.
- **Verify "Pre-Quarantined" Claims:** If a vendor charges extra for "Fully Quarantined" fish, request their specific protocol, duration, and medication list.
